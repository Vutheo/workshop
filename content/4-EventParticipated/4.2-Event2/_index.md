### Event Objectives

- Share practical experience in applying AI to learning, software development, and enterprise environments.
- Update participants on the latest trends in Generative AI, DevOps, and Cloud Computing.
- Introduce AI Agent solutions, Amazon Q, and Multi-Agent architectures in real-world applications.
- Create opportunities for networking and learning from professionals working in AI and Cloud Computing.

## Key Highlights

### Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

The first session emphasized the importance of providing **context** so AI can better understand the problem instead of relying only on prompts.

- Introduced the role of a **Platform Engineer** in building self-service platforms for developers.
- Explained that effective context should include goals, target users, coding standards, project documentation, and internal knowledge.
- Highlighted the importance of providing company-specific information instead of general knowledge already available to AI.
- Advised against copying prompts or source code from the Internet without understanding the context.
- Encouraged developing an **AI mindset**, viewing AI as a tool that enhances productivity rather than replaces human thinking.

### Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Q

The speaker introduced **Amazon Q** as an enterprise AI assistant capable of connecting with multiple business systems and automating workflows.

- Integrates with Microsoft Teams, Microsoft Office, Gmail, Google Calendar, Jira, Confluence, and AWS services.
- Supports AI Agents that can summarize meetings, analyze Excel data, generate dashboards, and send automated emails.
- Provides Business Intelligence, enterprise data chat, and workflow automation capabilities.
- Follows the AWS Shared Responsibility Model during deployment.
- Demonstrated practical use cases such as generating dashboards from Excel files, creating system prompts, and automating post-meeting tasks.

### AI Prompting, DevOps và Amazon CloudFront

This session shared practical experience in using AI efficiently together with AWS services.

- Explained how to build structured prompts using Goal, Context, and Format.
- Introduced Amazon CloudFront's Flat-rate Pricing model to improve cost predictability.
- Shared techniques for DDoS protection and SYN Proxy implementation.
- Demonstrated how CloudFront Functions can execute logic at the edge to reduce latency and improve application performance.

### Building UTMorpho From Idea To Reality

The speakers shared their experience participating in the **LotusHack Hackathon**, where they developed a product within 36 hours.

- Built an AI-powered tool that generates and edits user interfaces based on real-world requirements.
- Designed AI Agents capable of generating HTML/CSS interfaces with live editing support.
- Discussed challenges such as limited tokens, tight deadlines, and feature prioritization.
- Emphasized focusing on core value, effective teamwork, and time management.

### Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings

This session explained why Large Language Models may still produce different outputs even when **Temperature = 0**.

- Introduced the basic principles of LLMs and the Temperature parameter.
- Explained how GPU floating-point precision and parallel computation affect inference results.
- Discussed inference optimization techniques used by cloud providers.
- Compared outputs between cloud-hosted models and self-hosted models.
- Suggested solutions such as Structured Output, JSON Mode, multiple inference runs, and designing systems capable of handling output variations.

### Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent Systems

The final session introduced a Multi-Agent architecture for startup credit scoring.

- Different AI Agents were assigned specialized tasks such as financial analysis, market research, founder evaluation, and risk assessment.
- Emphasized designing AI systems based on real business requirements.
- Highlighted the importance of security, compliance, prompt injection prevention, audit trails, and human-in-the-loop validation.
- Encouraged AI engineers to strengthen their knowledge of software engineering, infrastructure, security, and ROI evaluation.
